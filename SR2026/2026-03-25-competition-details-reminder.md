Hello,

The competition is just a couple of weeks away. We hope you're ready and excited for the competition. Don't worry if your robot doesn't look perfect - many teams make great progress during the early stages of the event.

## Quick Reminders

Our last few emails contained lots of information for the event, but to reiterate some of the important ones:

- The Competition is a two day event, from Saturday morning until the finals on Sunday afternoon
- If you expect to arrive after **9:45am** on Saturday, please let us know
- We will be taking **photos and video** throughout the event, if you would prefer not to appear in these please make this known to reception when you arrive
- If you haven't already, please [RSVP to the event](https://forms.gle/9vNgYY6TY2LpYCSz8) and let us know details of your team.
- Please ensure you **bring your entire robotics kit** and all equipment, materials and tools you may need to work on your robot
- Be sure to **check the [rules][rules]** and ensure your robot is safe to avoid any last minute surprises
- Make sure your robot follows the [safety regulations][safety-regulations] and [design rules][design-rules], else it may not be able to compete

Please make sure you read the emails, and let us know if you have any questions or concerns.

If your team can't make the event in April, or you are otherwise unable to continue in Student Robotics, please let us know as soon as possible.

## Rules update

We've released a small update to the rules. It does not change the game, and instead is focused on the awards:

- We've introduced an additional 4th place edible prize, ensuring all finalists receive an award
- We've elevated the Rookie Award to a trophy, to better recognise the achievement

These rules are not available on our website.

We look forward to seeing you all at the Competition!
